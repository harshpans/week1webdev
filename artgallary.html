The Mona Lisa is a renowned half-length portrait painted by Leonardo da Vinci during 1503-1506. It's considered a masterpiece of the Italian Renaissance. The painting, also known as La Gioconda, depicts the wife of Francesco del Giocondo and is oil on wood, measuring 77 x 53 cm. Its fame stems from da Vinci's skillful use of sfumato, creating a realistic and softly sculptural face for the subject. The mystery behind Mona Lisa's smile and her captivating gaze contribute to its iconic status, making it one of the most famous paintings globally.

The Girl with a Pearl Earring is a 1665 oil painting by Dutch painter Johannes Vermeer. It is generally considered one of the most famous paintings in the world and is regarded as one of Vermeer's masterpieces. The painting depicts a young woman wearing a blue turban and a pearl earring. She is looking over her shoulder at the viewer with a slight smile. The painting is notable for its use of light and color, as well as its realistic depiction of the woman's skin and clothing.

The "Whistler's Mother" is a famous painting by American artist James Abbott McNeill Whistler, created in 1871. The painting depicts Whistler's mother, Anna McNeill Whistler, sitting in a simple wooden chair, facing the viewer. She is wearing a black dress and a white cap, and her hands are folded in her lap. The painting is known for its muted colors and simple composition, which highlights the serene and contemplative expression on Whistler's mother's face
